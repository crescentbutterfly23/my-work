02

Logo system

The CC monogram anchors the identity. Use the primary lockup where space allows, and the seals for stamps, packaging, and social. When placed on a dark background, apply the gold outline treatment.

Primary · on Casa Black
Oval badge · on Antique Gold

✓ Do

  • Keep clear space around the mark of at least the height of the CC monogram.
  • Apply the gold outline when the logo sits on a dark background.
  • Lead with the gold line-art seals for a refined, heritage feel.

✗ Don't

  • Recolor the monogram outside the brand palette.
  • Stretch, rotate, or add drop shadows to the lockup.
  • Place the mark on busy imagery without a scrim or clear space.

Mascot & character

A young ambassador of heritage, craftsmanship, and hospitality. Warm, proud, and always ready to share something made with care.

Lead with the line art.

The gold line-art character is the primary expression across packaging, seals, and web. The full-color version is reserved for feature moments. When the mascot sits on a dark background, apply the gold outline.

Primary · Line Art

Full body and bust, in Antique Gold. The default across most applications.

Secondary · Full Color

Hero and feature moments only. Use with intention, not everywhere.

Small size · Icon

Below the mascot's legible size, use the CC monogram seal instead.

07

Voice & taglines

Warm and proud, never loud. We make the familiar feel extraordinary.

Bringing Tradition Forward Primary tagline
From our table to yours. Signature sign-off

Sounds like

  • “Handcrafted. Heritage-inspired. Made for Miami.”
  • “Crispy outside. Creamy inside. Unforgettable always.”
  • “Miami born. Heritage driven.”

Avoid

  • Hype, slang, or discount-driven urgency.
  • Long paragraphs where a warm line will do.
  • Anything that feels mass-produced or impersonal.
